# Typography Inc.: Monopoly/Arcade Engagement Mechanics (Brainstorm)

## 1) Section “Deed / Receipt” Collectibles
- **What it is:** A tiny “Property Deed” (history sections) or “Bank Receipt” (licensing sections) that certifies the key takeaway in 3–5 lines.
- **Where it appears:** End of each Monopoly-square section (GO, Property 1–3, The Bank, Rent & Utilities, Free Parking).
- **Why it improves learning:** Summary + self-explanation prompt strengthens consolidation and supports quick rereads (recognition → recall).
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** Collapsed by default (“Claim deed”), expands only on tap; optional “Add to binder” button shown only after expansion.

## 2) “Pay Rent” Micro-Retrieval Checks
- **What it is:** A 10-second “rent payment” where readers answer 1 sharp question to proceed (e.g., “Who owns what in this era?” / “What right does this license grant?”).
- **Where it appears:** Immediately after each major widget or section wrap-up (timeline slider, quiz, OpenType lab).
- **Why it improves learning:** Retrieval practice + immediate feedback beats rereading for durable memory; keeps attention anchored to the learning goal.
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** Only one question; appears as a small inline row at the section end; can be skipped (“Pay later”) without blocking reading.

## 3) Stamp Book Micro-Achievements (“Arcade Stamps”)
- **What it is:** Small themed stamps like **“Kerning Clerk”**, **“License Literate”**, **“Feature Flipper”** earned by completing existing widget missions.
- **Where it appears:** Right next to each widget’s completion moment (type quiz result, anatomy explorer fully clicked, OpenType lab successful toggle combo, pairing sandbox saved pair).
- **Why it improves learning:** Competence signaling + goal gradient effect; encourages finishing interactions and revisiting to “complete the set.”
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** Stamp appears only after interaction; stays tiny and local; a collapsed “Stamp book” link lives at section ends only.

## 4) Property “Upgrades” as Mastery Markers (Houses/Hotels)
- **What it is:** Each section’s square quietly “upgrades” from empty lot → house → hotel as readers complete its core interaction(s).
- **Where it appears:** At the section title line for Property squares and The Bank/Rent & Utilities.
- **Why it improves learning:** Visible progress supports persistence and return visits; upgrades map to mastery rather than mere scrolling.
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** Only a 1-icon change near the heading; no global board; upgrade animation only on first earn.

## 5) Token Persona Playthrough (Choose Your “Player Token”)
- **What it is:** Readers pick a token (e.g., **Indie Designer**, **Startup PM**, **Publisher/Editor**) that lightly re-frames examples and prompts.
- **Where it appears:** At GO (start) + echoed in the Jail/Audit capstone scenario.
- **Why it improves learning:** Transfer and motivation—people remember rules better when they’re tied to a role and plausible decisions.
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** One-time choice, collapsible; no constant banner—just occasional one-line callouts (“As a Publisher, what do you need to check?”).

## 6) Chance/Community Chest as Spaced-Replay “Draws”
- **What it is:** A larger deck of replayable cards that mix myth-busting, mini-scenarios, and “spot the license red flag” moments.
- **Where it appears:** At natural breath points (between Property squares; after Rent & Utilities).
- **Why it improves learning:** Interleaving + spaced repetition; repeated short exposures build licensing intuition.
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** Collapsed stacks (“Draw 1 card”); limit to 1–2 draws per section; “Shuffle for later” option for rereads.

## 7) “Auction House” Challenge for Type Pairing (Explain Your Bid)
- **What it is:** In the pairing sandbox, readers “bid” on a pairing by choosing *why it works* (contrast, x-height harmony, mood, licensing fit) in one sentence.
- **Where it appears:** Type pairing sandbox.
- **Why it improves learning:** Forces articulation (generative learning) and connects aesthetics to constraints (licensing literacy + design rationale).
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** Optional “Place bid” button; appears only after a pairing is made; short feedback (“Solid contrast—watch license scope”).

## 8) OpenType “Power-Up Combos” (Feature Lab Missions)
- **What it is:** Bite-size missions like “Make body text calmer” or “Make headers more ‘premium’” using feature toggles, then name the feature(s).
- **Where it appears:** OpenType feature lab.
- **Why it improves learning:** Application + labeling builds usable knowledge (not just toggling); supports future recall in real typography work.
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** Missions tucked under a “Try a combo” disclosure; reward appears only on completion.

## 9) “Bank Ledger” Decision Slips (Tiny Cost/Risk Reflections)
- **What it is:** After key licensing sections, readers pick 1 of 3 “ledger slips” like **Low risk / Medium / High** with a one-line justification.
- **Where it appears:** The Bank + Rent & Utilities sections.
- **Why it improves learning:** Synthesis + metacognitive calibration; turns abstract license terms into decision-making practice.
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** One-tap choice; optional “Why?” expands; results stored only in the end binder, not shown persistently.

## 10) End-of-Guide Capstone: “Jail / Audit Letter” Boss Challenge
- **What it is:** A narrative scenario where the reader is “audited” and must assemble the right documents/answers to get released (or negotiate a settlement).
- **Where it appears:** Finale section (Jail square), pulling evidence from the timeline, quiz knowledge, OpenType lab, and licensing sections.
- **Why it improves learning:** High-transfer assessment (case-based reasoning) + integration across the guide; makes the ending feel earned.
- **How to keep it non-distracting:** Collapsed until “Start audit”; can be completed in 3–5 minutes; “Hint” is optional and only appears after a wrong choice.

## Top 3 (Value Add)
1. **Jail / Audit Letter Boss Challenge** (integration + rewarding ending)
2. **Deed / Receipt Collectibles** (completion + reread utility)
3. **Pay Rent Micro-Retrieval Checks** (durable learning with minimal footprint)

## 3 Runner-Ups
- **Chance/Community Chest spaced-replay draws**
- **Stamp Book micro-achievements**
- **Token Persona playthrough**

## Suggested Lightweight Arcade Loop
- **Land** (enter a square) → **Learn** (short story beat + key concept) → **Play** (existing widget) → **Pay Rent** (1-question retrieval) → **Claim** (collapsed deed/receipt + optional stamp) → **Draw** (optional Chance/Chest card for replay) → repeat, culminating in the **Audit Letter** boss that “uses your binder” to prove mastery.
