# Campaign Spec Template

Use this as the task-specific recipe card for one campaign.

Do not leave key fields blank. If a field cannot be completed, mark it `MISSING` and resolve it before generation.

## Filling Out This Template

If this template has blank fields, help the user complete it before doing any other work. Use the interview guide below.

If this template is already complete, skip the interview and use it as campaign context.

### Interview Guide

Ask questions in small groups of 2-3. Wait for answers before continuing. Do not overwhelm the user. Use plain language.

**Start with:** "I'll help you fill out the campaign spec. I'll ask some questions in small batches, then put your answers into the template. Let's start with the basics."

**Group 1 — Campaign basics:**

- What is this campaign called?
- What is the primary objective — what should this campaign make happen?
- What counts as a win?
- What is the primary CTA?
- What should this campaign explicitly not optimize for?

**Group 2 — Product and audience:**

- What product or offer is this campaign about?
- Is there an approved short name?
- What is the launch status?
- Are there any approved or banned ways to describe this product?
- Who is the primary audience? Is there a secondary audience?
- What does this audience need from their workflow that this campaign connects to?

**Group 3 — Business context:**

- Why does this campaign exist?
- Why now — what changed, launched, or became urgent?
- What market tension or workflow bottleneck should this campaign lead with?
- What should the audience know, feel, or do after reading?

**Group 4 — Deliverables:**

- Which channels and assets are required for this campaign?

**Group 5 — Constraints and risks:**

- Are there any campaign-specific things the copy must do?
- Are there any campaign-specific things the copy must not do?
- What would sound polished but wrong for this campaign?
- What should a copyeditor not need to rewrite?

After the interview, output the completed template with all fields filled in. Mark any fields the user could not answer as `MISSING`.

For the Campaign Spine section, tell the user: "The campaign spine is usually built after the spec is complete. You can use the spine builder prompt to fill that section, or complete it manually after the campaign manager approves the spine."

## Pre-Service Intake

These questions are answered by the campaign manager in the Asana brief intake form before work begins. Transfer the answers here.

1. What outcome do we actually want?
2. What would count as wrong even if the copy looks polished?
3. What do we know that an outside writer would not know?
4. What is the hard part of this campaign?
5. What is different about this campaign from the last similar campaign?

If any answer is missing or vague, resolve it with the campaign manager before proceeding.

## Decomposition

Do not generate everything at once. Use this staged workflow.

### Stage 0: Source Prep

Allowed AI tasks before approvals:

- summarize noisy source materials
- extract candidate proof and workflow notes
- identify gaps or conflicts
- propose `UNAPPROVED` spine options

Required output:

- source summary
- missing-info list
- claim-risk list
- example coverage gaps

Check before moving on:

- Are the required inputs present?
- Are approval-sensitive claims documented?
- Are the right channels covered by approved examples?

### Stage 1: Campaign Spine

Input:

- completed spec
- source-of-truth materials

Output:

- core positioning statement
- market tension or workflow bottleneck
- product-as-response statement
- proof anchors
- required workflow tie-ins
- optional workflow tie-ins
- core message to preserve across channels
- messaging angles to avoid
- channel angle map

Check before moving on:

- Is the strategic angle correct?
- Are the proof anchors approved and current?
- Is the CTA correct?
- Has the campaign manager approved the spine?

### Stage 2: Long-Form Core Assets

Input:

- approved campaign spine

Output:

- prospect email
- client email
- newsletter feature or other core long-form asset

Check before moving on:

- Do the long-form assets agree on angle and proof?
- Are workflow tie-ins useful rather than generic?
- Are any claims risky or undersourced?
- Do the drafts stay within the approved claims wording?

### Stage 3: Derived Channel Assets

Input:

- approved long-form assets

Output:

- rep email
- social assets
- ads
- secondary page copy

Check before moving on:

- Did any channel drift from the spine?
- Did any channel add a new claim?
- Is each asset in the correct format?
- Does each asset follow its approved example patterns without copying them?

### Stage 4: QA Pass

Input:

- full draft set
- `04-qa-checklists.md`

Output:

- final deliverables
- list of issues fixed
- list of unresolved questions
- estimated editorial rewrite burden

- Has Production confirmed the packet is ready to run?

## Acceptance Criteria

These should be checkable by someone other than the original requester.

- The campaign uses the approved product name consistently in every asset.
- Every long-form asset includes an approved proof point within the opening section.
- Every required long-form asset includes at least one explicit workflow application relevant to the audience.
- Social and secondary assets preserve the same strategic angle as the long-form spine.
- No asset introduces unsupported claims, outdated naming, or unapproved positioning.
- Every asset matches its required channel format.
- The selected claims match the approved wording or qualifiers in the claims ledger.
- Every selected channel has at least one approved example or an explicit human-authored channel spec.

## Definition of Done

This campaign is done when:

- every required asset is drafted
- every asset passes the relevant QA checklist
- all proof points and claims are sourced to approved materials
- unresolved escalation items are either answered or explicitly flagged
- the full campaign reads as one strategy adapted across channels, not separate pieces written independently
- the copyeditor is not rewriting strategy, claims, or channel structure from scratch
- the campaign manager recognizes the output as aligned to the intended campaign direction
- the copywriter and copyeditor would both recognize the output as meeting the intended standard
