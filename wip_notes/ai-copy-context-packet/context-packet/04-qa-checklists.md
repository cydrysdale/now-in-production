# QA Checklists

Use these checklists as acceptance criteria and as a lightweight evaluation system.

Do not approve by vibe alone.

## Campaign-Level Checklist

- [ ] The readiness gate was passed before generation started.
- [ ] The same market tension appears across the campaign.
- [ ] The same positioning angle appears across the campaign.
- [ ] Proof anchors are consistent across channels.
- [ ] No channel introduces a conflicting message.
- [ ] Product naming is consistent and approved.
- [ ] Audience relevance is clear in every asset.
- [ ] Claims are backed by approved source material.
- [ ] Claims wording stays inside the claims ledger allowances.
- [ ] Time-sensitive facts are still within their freshness window.

## Readiness Gate

All answers must be `yes` before final copy generation:

- [ ] Is the audience explicit?
- [ ] Is the objective explicit?
- [ ] Is the CTA explicit?
- [ ] Is the campaign spine approved by the campaign manager?
- [ ] Has the copyeditor approved the editorial quality bar and failure-mode coverage?
- [ ] Is the claims ledger complete for this campaign?
- [ ] Are source-of-truth documents ranked?
- [ ] Are approved examples available for each selected channel?
- [ ] Are any current-market facts still within their refresh window?
- [ ] Has Production confirmed the packet is ready to run?

## Prospect Email Checklist

- [ ] Subject line fits the required format and length guidance.
- [ ] Opening establishes market tension or a workflow problem before product.
- [ ] An approved proof anchor appears in paragraph 1 or 2.
- [ ] The body includes a practical workflow tie-in.
- [ ] The CTA is clear and within format rules.
- [ ] The email reads as useful to the target buyer, not generic brand copy.

## Client Email Checklist

- [ ] The email contains a `Now you can...` line or equivalent action-forward framing.
- [ ] The body reinforces practical ongoing use or monitoring where relevant.
- [ ] The email preserves the campaign spine rather than inventing a new angle.
- [ ] The workflow tie-in is concrete.
- [ ] The CTA is clear and format-compliant.

## Sales and CS Rep Email Checklist

- [ ] The tone is warmer than marketing copy without becoming casual or vague.
- [ ] A direct link reference is present.
- [ ] The close is soft, not pushy.
- [ ] The message still preserves the approved positioning and proof.

## Registration Page Checklist

- [ ] The page makes the audience and value explicit above the fold.
- [ ] The copy explains why this asset matters now.
- [ ] The page includes approved proof, scope, or speaker credibility.
- [ ] The CTA is clear and format-compliant.

## Invite Email Checklist

- [ ] The copy leads with audience value before logistics.
- [ ] The timing or urgency is clear without hype.
- [ ] The asset, event, or speaker credibility is supported by approved proof.
- [ ] The CTA is clear and format-compliant.

## On-Demand Page Checklist

- [ ] The copy makes the takeaway value explicit.
- [ ] The reason to watch now is clear.
- [ ] The copy uses approved proof or scope anchors.
- [ ] The CTA is clear and format-compliant.

## Thank-You Email Checklist

- [ ] The email delivers immediate value or access clearly.
- [ ] The next step is explicit when relevant.
- [ ] The copy preserves the approved campaign positioning.
- [ ] The email does not introduce new claims or angles.

## Newsletter Feature Ad Checklist

- [ ] The headline is aligned to the campaign spine.
- [ ] Market tension is present.
- [ ] A proof anchor is present.
- [ ] The value proposition is concrete, not abstract.
- [ ] The CTA is clear.

## Social Asset Checklist

- [ ] The post keeps the same strategic angle as the email spine.
- [ ] The hook is strong without becoming hype.
- [ ] The proof point is preserved where format allows.
- [ ] The CTA is appropriate for the platform.
- [ ] Platform constraints are followed.

## Risk Checklist

Escalate if any answer is `yes`.

- [ ] Does the copy imply performance, financial, or legal claims not explicitly approved?
- [ ] Does the copy compare against competitors using unsupported or dated information?
- [ ] Does the copy rely on a proof point that does not appear in the approved source material?
- [ ] Does the copy use a legacy product name, retired tagline, or stale positioning?
- [ ] Does the copy sound polished while still feeling strategically off?
- [ ] Does the copy exceed the allowed wording or qualifiers in the claims ledger?
- [ ] Does the copy rely on a source that is outside its freshness window?

## Lightweight Eval Rubric

Score each campaign on a 1-5 scale for:

- Strategic alignment
- Proof quality
- Workflow relevance
- Channel consistency
- Format compliance
- Editorial burden

Suggested interpretation for `Editorial burden`:

- `5` = copyeditor makes minor line edits only
- `4` = copyeditor trims or sharpens, but does not rewrite the strategy
- `3` = copyeditor rewrites one section or channel substantially
- `2` = copyeditor rewrites multiple sections for strategy, claims, or voice
- `1` = draft is unusable without major reconstruction

Use the scores to compare campaigns over time.

## Copyeditor Burden Check

- [ ] The copyeditor would not need to rewrite the strategic angle.
- [ ] The copyeditor would not need to replace unsupported claims.
- [ ] The copyeditor would not need to rebuild the asset structure from scratch.
- [ ] The copyeditor would mainly adjust wording, clarity, or cadence.

## Suggested Gold-Set Eval

For recurring campaign types, maintain 3-5 benchmark cases:

- a filled campaign spec
- a claims ledger
- an approved examples file
- a known-good output set

Re-run these cases when the packet changes or when you switch models.

## Suggested Regression Checks

Run these checks when comparing a new campaign to previous work:

- Is the writing getting more generic over time?
- Are workflow tie-ins becoming formulaic?
- Are proof anchors drifting later in the copy?
- Is social drifting further from email strategy?
- Are revisions repeatedly fixing the same issue? If so, update the stable rules or campaign spec.
