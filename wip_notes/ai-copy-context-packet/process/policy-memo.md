# Policy Memo: Copy AI Packet Ownership — Draft

> **Status: Draft exploration — not an approved policy.**
> This memo is part of an active workflow exploration. It proposes an ownership model for discussion, not for immediate adoption.

To keep one-off AI prompts from turning into unofficial standards, this memo proposes that copy AI packets be managed the same way as other creative enablement assets: the copy function would own the content standard, Production would own the system and governance, and anything adopted as a shared standard would require lead review before rollout.

## Ownership Lanes

### Campaign Manager

Owns the campaign spine.

- defines or approves campaign direction, audience priority, CTA, and core positioning
- approves the campaign spine before drafting begins
- resolves strategic conflicts that affect the campaign argument across channels

### Copywriter

Owns the strategic content standard.

- defines messaging strategy, audience framing, CTA, and channel intent
- defines what good output looks like for the use case
- supplies or approves source inputs, proof points, and examples
- translates the approved campaign spine into reusable copy patterns and channel messaging

### Copyeditor

Owns the editorial quality standard.

- defines the editorial quality bar for clarity, consistency, usage, and brand fit
- identifies polished-but-wrong failure modes and recurring editorial issues
- helps define anti-patterns, QA checks, and rewrite triggers
- reviews whether generated copy is publishable without rescue-level rewriting

### Production

Owns the system and governance mechanics.

- packet structure and prompt workflow design
- SOPs and HITL checkpoints
- template architecture, metadata, and naming conventions
- versioning, readiness gates, QA gates, publishing, and maintenance

### B&C or Marketing Leads

Own rollout governance for shared standards.

- review any packet, workflow, or library intended for team-wide adoption
- approve rollout before it is positioned as the default standard

## Why This Structure

AI packets are configuration-like assets. Small changes can materially affect output. They should be treated as systemized enablement with clear ownership, review, versioning, and intentional publishing, not as one-off docs, chat messages, or DMs.

For copy specifically:

- the campaign manager owns whether the campaign spine is strategically correct
- the copywriter owns whether the message is strategically correct
- the copyeditor owns whether the draft is editorially sound and brand-ready
- Production owns whether the packet is reusable, controlled, and safe to scale

## Standard Adoption Rule

No packet should be treated as a shared standard unless:

- the campaign manager has approved the campaign spine model where applicable
- the copywriter has approved the strategic content standard
- the copyeditor has approved the editorial quality standard
- Production has systemized the workflow and QA
- leads have reviewed it if it is intended for broader rollout
